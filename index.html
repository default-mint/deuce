The Opening Note

On the long match.

A match is rarely decided in the first set. It is decided in the hour after, when the legs go heavy and the serve toss drifts and the mind starts negotiating with the body. That hour is the real game. Everything we make is built for the player who is still there when it arrives.

We are not interested in a wall of gear. A player reaches for a small, familiar set of things: a grip that holds when the hands are damp, a dampener that quiets the frame, a band that keeps sweat off the strings. A towel. A bag that lives in the car. A visor for the afternoon sun. Seven pieces, chosen once, used for years.

And then there is the part of the long match that no towel can fix. Tired legs, slow recovery between points, the warm-up that takes a little longer than it used to. That is where Circulation comes in, and we will be honest about exactly what it is and is not, later in this issue.

What follows is the kit and the formula, laid out the way we think about them: plainly, in order, with the price in view. Read it like a clubhouse noticeboard, not a catalog. Then pick the one or two things you will actually reach for, and get back on court.

The Feature · The Formula

Circulation,
for the back half.

A blood-flow supplement built around the demands of tennis. It supports circulation, leg freshness, and stamina across long matches and third sets.

Most gear stops at the surface. A grip helps the hand, a band helps the sweat, a towel helps between games. None of it reaches the part of the match that actually wears you down: the legs, the recovery, the slow climb back to fresh after a long rally.

Circulation is our answer to that. It is a daily supplement, taken before play, formulated to support healthy blood flow so your legs feel fresher deeper into a match and your warm-up comes a little easier between games. It is not a stimulant and it is not a shortcut. It is one honest piece of the long-match picture.

We name every ingredient and print nothing we cannot stand behind.
